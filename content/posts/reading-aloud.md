Every editor I have ever trusted eventually told me the same thing: *read it aloud.*
I nodded. I took it to heart. I immediately forgot.

## Why it works

Prose has a body. It has breath, cadence, tempo. The eye is a generous reader; it will
skim over a clumsy clause because it can already see the end of the sentence. The mouth
will not. The mouth runs out of air at the exact word where the sentence should have
ended.

## What I listen for

- **Breath.** Where am I running out of it? Probably a sentence that needs cutting.
- **Stumbles.** Where did I trip on my own word order? Re-order it.
- **Musicality.** Is there a paragraph where every sentence has the same shape? Vary them.
- **Pace.** Am I speaking faster in the parts I'm unsure about? Those are the parts to
  rewrite, not the parts to rush past.

## The trick that finally made it stick

I record it. Voice memos, phone mic, on the walk from the kitchen to the desk. Playing
it back is the moment of truth. You can't self-deceive when the recording is speaking
your own sentences back at you.
